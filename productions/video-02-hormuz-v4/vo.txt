## 09 why-hormuz
Step back. Why does one waterway price everything? Scale. About twenty million barrels of oil move through Hormuz every single day. That is a fifth of world consumption and more than a quarter of all seaborne oil, plus about a fifth of global L N G. There is no pipeline network that replaces it. Think of it as the busiest toll booth in the global economy, with no service road around it. The bypass routes that do exist are limited, and they are already in use. So when the cost of that chokepoint rises, there is no relief valve on the supply side. The price does not negotiate. It just moves. That is why one tanker strike shows up in your energy stocks, your airline tickets, and the inflation print the Fed is staring at.
