## 2 Why

Chain (≤4 links, each fired Monday):
**9th consecutive night of US strikes on Iran + Houthi naval blockade declared against Saudi
Arabia (both 07-20, CNN/NPR/NBC)** → supply-risk premium extended: Brent to 91.42 July high →
**oil up ~20% MTD = an inflation shock in the pipe: US10Y +4.5bp to 4.594, pressing the July
high** → duration + risk repriced: the equity gap-up sold from the open, SPX closed on the lows.
Midmorning Iran diplomatic signal (Baghaei, CNBC 07-20) is why the OIL premium faded intraday —
but equities kept selling anyway, which is the tell (see §4).

## 3 Paid / hurt

- **Paid:** energy longs — XLE +0.45% to a new July high on a red tape; the breakout named
  Friday completed its second close. Producers hold a policy-and-war floor.
- **Hurt:** duration-heavy tech — NDX gave back a full +0.9% gap; the broad tape — Dow −0.59%;
  anyone who bought Monday's open — the gap-and-fade trapped the bounce buyers.
- **Mechanism each:** war premium feeds crude → crude feeds inflation expectations → yields at
  4.59% raise the discount rate on long-duration growth → rallies get sold until the rates
  pressure lifts.
