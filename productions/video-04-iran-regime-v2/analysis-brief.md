## 2 Why did it move?

Shock class: geopolitical supply-route risk. The bounded transmission map is: confirmed danger to commercial navigation around Hormuz → higher freight, insurance, delay, and compliance risk → higher delivered-energy risk → inflation and rate sensitivity → relative support for energy and pressure on long-duration growth assets. The map is an inference, not a claim that the conflict caused every tick.

Primary event sources are the [IMO confirmed-incident register](https://www.imo.org/en/mediacentre/hottopics/pages/middle-east-highlighted-incidents.aspx), the [IMO Council statement on vital shipping lanes](https://www.imo.org/en/mediacentre/pressbriefings/pages/imo-council-reaffirms-commitment-to-protecting-vital-shipping-lanes.aspx), and [U.S. Treasury's July 15 sanctions action](https://home.treasury.gov/news/press-releases/sb0564). ## 3 Who gets paid, who gets hurt?

- Paid while the route premium holds: energy producers and broad energy exposure. XLE's live gain while Brent was lower is partial confirmation that producer equities retained a war premium.
- Hurt while yields and the dollar stay firm: rate-sensitive, long-duration growth exposure. The Nasdaq 100 was the weakest major asset in the fixed dashboard at the cutoff.
- Operationally exposed: vessel owners, cargo interests, refiners, airlines, and other fuel-intensive businesses. The mechanism is freight, insurance, delay, and delivered-fuel cost; this brief does not claim a same-day price move for any uncaptured asset.
- Sanctions exposure is distinct from physical supply loss. Treasury's July 14 and July 15 actions add financing, transport, procurement, and compliance pressure, but a designation is not proof that a barrel disappeared.

## 5 What's already priced?

Brent closed July 10 at 75.22 and July 13 at 83.31, a 10.75% repricing in one completed session. It then traded as high as 87.55 on July 14 and closed 85.15, before closing 85.07 on July 15. The immediate route-risk premium is therefore already visible in the chart. The July 16 live bar showed no additional breakout despite the updated official shipping record. A new leg needs either a verified change in physical navigation, damage, supply access, or a completed technical break; another dramatic headline alone is not enough evidence.

## 6 What's the map?

- Base / selective repricing: Brent holds 83.31–87.55, XLE holds 56.50–57.30, VIX stays below 17.56, and Nasdaq remains weaker than the S&P. Trigger: official shipping risk remains elevated without a verified step-change in physical flows.
- Escalation: an official source confirms additional navigation disruption, vessel damage, or lost supply; Brent then completes a daily close above 87.55, XLE clears 57.30, and VIX clears 17.56. The event and prices must agree.
- Relief: an official source confirms improved safe passage or de-escalation; Brent completes below 83.31, XLE loses 56.50, and Nasdaq 100 reclaims the prior 29502.60 close. This is the route-premium unwind case.

These are decision ranges plus event triggers, not forecasts.
