## 03 yaml-receipt

## 04 falsifiable-card
Because a strategy has to be written as exact, falsifiable rules before it can be tested. When does a level count as swept? Which candle closes the order block? Ten ICT traders will give ten different answers — and every one of them is unfalsifiable in the moment and obvious in hindsight. If it can't be written as code, it can't be tested. If it can't be tested, it can't be disproven. And a method that can't be disproven isn't a method. It's a horoscope with a Discord server.

## 05 three-drawings
And to be fair — this is not saying ICT traders are stupid. It's saying the famous name can't be pinned down honestly. Everyone who claims they backtested ICT actually tested their own private interpretation. A different strategy, wearing the brand.

## 06 concept-cards
So what CAN be coded? Ten textbook concepts, each with decades of literature behind it. Donchian channel breakouts and breakdowns — the nineteen sixties trend rules. The golden cross. EMA momentum. RSI mean reversion, and its momentum cousin. Volatility squeeze breakouts. ATR expansion. Inside bar compression. Bollinger band mean reversion. Boring? Maybe. Testable? Completely. Every one is exact rules, no interpretation, no vibes.

## 07 funnel-capture
Each concept gets bred into thousands of parameter variants — eight thousand and forty two candidates per market. Then the funnel: parse check, look-ahead bias check, fixture evaluation, duplicate signal kill, and every survivor is replayed twice — if the two runs don't match exactly, it dies. Across forty six market scopes: three hundred forty nine thousand nine hundred fourteen bred. Two hundred twenty five thousand nine hundred ninety six unique survivors. One hundred eleven thousand nine hundred sixty six admitted.

## 08 untested-badges
And here's the part that matters. Admitted does not mean works. Admitted means honest enough to be worth torturing. In this system every single one of them carries the same label: UNTESTED. Compare that to every backtest site on earth, where one green equity curve gets called profitable.

## 09 gauntlet-phases
Because next comes the gauntlet. Twelve phases. Intake baseline. Out-of-sample retest. Session timing stress. Cost stress at two to three times real slippage. Monte Carlo parameter perturbation — two hundred simulations per candidate. Monte Carlo trade shuffle — if your edge disappears when the trade order changes, it was luck. Walk-forward with frozen parameters. A walk-forward correlation gate. Final out-of-sample. And a governance battery: deflated Sharpe ratio, probability of backtest overfitting, combinatorial cross-validation. Each phase exists because somebody, somewhere, got fooled without it.
