## 4. Mechanics — why it matters
- EIA baseline (pub 2025-06-16, 2024 data, PRE-event): Hormuz ~20M b/d ≈ 20% of global
  petroleum liquids, >25% of seaborne oil, ~19-20% of global LNG (93% of Qatar's, 96%
  of UAE's).
- IEA (via The National 07/13): global oil output still 9.4M b/d BELOW pre-war level;
  global supply rebounded +4.1M b/d in June to 98.8M b/d. IEA: "Renewed exchanges of
  fire in the Gulf this week highlight the risks of not reaching a lasting peace
  agreement, which is a must for the normalisation in oil markets."
- Transit counts (never smooth these — different windows):
  - 9 vessels Sat 18:00 GMT–Sun 06:00 GMT, 4 of them Iranian-flagged; 6 vessels the
    Thu-Fri overnight window; 18-22/day earlier in July; ~130/day peacetime
    (Al Jazeera 07/13).
  - CNN 07/13: "Fourteen vessels — half of them Iranian — transited yesterday."
  - Lloyd's List 07/10: only 3 fuel tankers actively broadcasting positions in the
    waterway (via CBS liveblog).
  - CENTCOM 07/12 graphic: 140+ ships in last 7 days — vastly higher than tracker
    counts; frame as "US military says" vs "independent trackers show".
- War-risk insurance: rates 2-6% of vessel value (peak ~10% at height of conflict,
  drifting under 2% before this flare-up) — The National 06/03 + Insurance Journal
  07/09. Insurance Journal 07/09: brokers see FEWER quote requests since ceasefire
  broke down — owners not even asking; visible traffic "virtually ground to a halt".
  Fairway (2026): war-risk adds ~$1.5-4.5M per Hormuz voyage.
- New economic layer: Trump's proposed 20% toll on cargo value for Hormuz passage
  (Bloomberg/CNBC/AJ 07/13) — if implemented, changes shipping economics on ~20% of
  world oil; Iran says it will respond to any US "management" of the strait.
