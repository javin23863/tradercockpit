## 05 why-hormuz
Why one waterway moves everything. The American Energy Information Administration's baseline: about twenty million barrels of oil per day pass through Hormuz — roughly one fifth of everything the planet consumes, and more than a quarter of all seaborne oil trade. Add one fifth of the world's liquefied natural gas, including ninety three percent of Qatar's exports. There is no pipe, no rail line, and no alternate route that replaces this strait. That is what the word chokepoint means.
