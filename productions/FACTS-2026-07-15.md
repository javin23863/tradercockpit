## 3. Top-story post brief

### Hook

**China grew 4.3% in Q2—but the headline hides two economies.**

### Verified facts

- NBS published the release at 10:00 China time / 09:00 ICT on July 15. It labels
  the GDP figures preliminary and calculates growth at constant prices.
  ([China NBS](https://www.stats.gov.cn/english/PressRelease/202607/t20260715_1964120.html))
- Q2 GDP grew 4.3% year over year and 0.9% quarter over quarter. Q1 growth was 5.0%
  year over year; first-half GDP grew 4.7%.
- First-half industrial value added rose 5.4%; equipment manufacturing rose 9.3%
  and high-tech manufacturing 13.3%.
- Consumer-goods retail sales rose 1.3%, while fixed-asset investment excluding
  rural households fell 5.7%. Real-estate development investment fell 18.0%.
- Goods trade remained strong: first-half exports rose 13.4% and imports 22.1%.

All figures above come from the same timestamped primary release:
[National Bureau of Statistics of China](https://www.stats.gov.cn/english/PressRelease/202607/t20260715_1964120.html).

`Slower GDP + weak retail/investment -> China demand expectations -> industrial
commodities and China-sensitive exporters -> global cyclicals.`

- The confirmed split is strong production/high-tech manufacturing against weak

  automation, EV/battery, and equipment supply chains may not follow the broader
  domestic-demand signal.
