## 2 Why did it move?

Shock class: demand shock. The transmission map is: NBS reports strong industrial/high-tech supply alongside weak domestic-demand proxies → investors separate export/industrial exposure from consumer/property exposure → mainland equities and CNH become the stress gauges → copper and FXI test whether the supply-side story is broadening beyond factories. The chain stops at four links; it is an analytical mechanism, not a claim that one release caused every bar.

Primary event source: [NBS national economic performance, 2026-07-15](https://www.stats.gov.cn/english/PressRelease/202607/t20260715_1964120.html).

## 3 Who gets paid, who gets hurt?

- Paid, if the production side holds: equipment and high-tech manufacturers, industrial exporters, and copper-linked supply chains. The mechanism is continued factory activity and goods demand rather than a broad consumer rebound.
- Hurt, if domestic demand remains soft: consumer-facing businesses, property exposure, and mainland equity baskets that carry the domestic-demand discount. The mechanism is slower household turnover and weaker investment appetite.
- FXI is not a clean winner label. Its July 15 close was stronger than the mainland's next cash session, so the ETF is a confirmation test, not a verdict.

## 4 What confirms it?

- Shanghai and Shenzhen confirm the domestic-equity stress: both completed July 16 lower, and both printed lows below their prior closes.
- FXI diverges: it closed July 15 higher at 34.28, while Shanghai and Shenzhen then closed July 16 lower. This divergence is promoted because it asks whether the market is pricing China as a broad demand story or as a selective large-cap / export story.
- USDCNH is a mild warning rather than a panic confirmation: the live bar is above the prior close but remains inside its recorded 6.76502–6.77210 range.
- Copper is higher in the live bar, which supports the industrial side but does not prove Chinese end-demand. The bar is still open.
- SPX closed July 15 at 7572.40 versus 7543.59 on July 14, so global equities do not confirm a synchronized liquidation regime.
