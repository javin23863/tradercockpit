## 05 transmission
The transmission map has four links. Strong industrial and high-tech output supports goods production. Goods production leans on exports and industrial inputs. Weak household and property demand limits the breadth of the recovery. Prices then decide whether investors pay for the supply story or discount it as excess capacity.

The release reports exports up thirteen point four percent and imports up twenty-two point one percent. Those are trade-flow facts, not a license to declare victory for exporters. They tell me the external channel is active. They do not tell me domestic demand has caught up.

That distinction is why copper, the yuan, and the mainland indices matter. If copper holds while cash indices wobble, the industrial channel is alive. If the yuan weakens and equity floors break, demand stress is travelling. If the U.S.-listed ETF ignores the mainland move, the market may be selecting large-cap or export exposure rather than buying the whole country.

This is not a prediction. It is a pressure chain with gauges.

## 10 paid-hurt
Who gets paid if production stays stronger than the customer? Equipment makers, high-tech manufacturers, exporters, and the industrial supply chain have the better first-order setup. Their mechanism is activity already visible in the official data.

Who gets hurt if domestic demand keeps lagging? Consumer exposure, property, and broad mainland equity baskets carry the harder burden. Their mechanism is simple: the customer has to spend or invest before the production story becomes a broad earnings story.

And FXI sits in the middle. It is not a moral winner. It is a market basket that held up after the release while the mainland indices then fell. That makes it useful as a confirmation gauge and dangerous as a shortcut.

My edge here is restraint. Production can be real and still fail to create a broad equity rally. Weak demand can be real and still fail to break copper on the first session. Both can be true at once.

That is not a contradiction. That is a market.
