form: **official sources confirm renewed danger to commercial shipping around the
Strait of Hormuz, followed by a new U.S. sanctions action; whether markets are

timestamped TradingView charts.** No official source reviewed establishes the
identity of the attackers, a current oil-flow volume, a current transit count, or a
causal market reaction.

Senate appearance, EIA's July 15 Weekly Petroleum Status Report, and the Federal
Reserve Beige Book had **not reached their official release times**. They are
catalysts, not results.

## Political/conflict eligibility chains

### 1. Strait of Hormuz — eligible lead

- **Timestamped official event:** IMO on July 8 condemned attacks over the prior two
  days against several ships transiting the Strait. On July 13 the IMO Council
  condemned attacks on civilian commercial ships and requested work toward a
  coordinated, sustainable return to unhindered navigation. On July 14 Treasury
  announced its shipping-network sanctions action and OFAC issued General License Z.
  [IMO July 8](https://www.imo.org/en/mediacentre/pressbriefings/pages/imo-secretary-general-condemns-new-attacks-on-ships-in-strait-of-hormuz.aspx),
  [IMO July 13](https://www.imo.org/en/mediacentre/pressbriefings/pages/imo-council-reaffirms-commitment-to-protecting-vital-shipping-lanes.aspx),
  [Treasury July 14](https://home.treasury.gov/news/press-releases/sb0562),
  [OFAC July 14](https://ofac.treasury.gov/recent-actions/20260714).
- **Explicit mechanism:** IMO's own framework is safe navigation and unhindered
  transit; Treasury's action directly covers an official-alleged network in oil
  exports, container shipping, and commodities trading. **Inference:** renewed
  danger plus sanctions enforcement may create freight, insurance, compliance, or
  oil-flow friction.
- **Exposed assets — inference:** crude/refined products; tanker and container
  shipping; marine insurance; energy producers; airlines and transports; Gulf and
  Asian import-sensitive assets; inflation-sensitive sovereign rates.
- **Confirm or deny:** later IMO navigation/incident notices; OFAC list, license, or
  enforcement updates; official port/operator notices; EIA stocks, imports,
  production, and product-supplied data; timestamped TradingView cross-asset charts.
  Do not substitute unverified vessel maps for an official current-flow statement.
- **Next official catalyst:** EIA WPSR after 10:30 ET on July 15; IMO and OFAC updates
  are event-driven; next scheduled EIA STEO is August 11.

### 2. Black Sea and Sea of Azov — watch-only, not standalone eligible

- **Timestamped official event:** IMO on July 13 reported a resurgence and deplored
  a series of attacks against civilian merchant ships over the prior week. It did
  not name an attacker.
- **Explicit mechanism:** IMO states that attacks threaten navigation and disrupt
  global supply chains. **Inference:** disruption risk can reach freight, marine
  insurance, and commodity-export logistics.
- **Exposed assets — inference:** dry-bulk shipping; marine insurance; wheat, corn,
  fertilizer, and regional energy/trade exposures.
- **Confirm or deny:** subsequent IMO notices; official port and export statistics;
  USDA supply reports; EU legal acts and licenses. A ship incident alone does not
  establish lost crop production or a named party's responsibility.
- **Next official catalyst:** IMO updates are event-driven. The reviewed statement
  supplies no specific dated follow-up, so this item fails the full political or
  conflict gate as a standalone production candidate.

### 5. UK/allied cyber advisory and sanctions — eligible fresh security event

- **Exposed assets — inference:** network equipment and cyber-security vendors;
  utilities and communications operators; financial services; critical-infrastructure
  insurers.
- **Confirm or deny:** subsequent NCSC/NSA/EU advisories, sanctions-list updates,
  named operator or regulator notices, and issuer filings.
- **Next official catalyst:** event-driven government advisory, attribution,
  sanctions, or named-operator disclosure.

   testimony, WPSR, and Beige Book were still future at this cutoff. Re-fetch their
   first-party pages before narration lock.

3. **Hormuz flow numbers are historical estimates.** The 20.7 and 14.6 million b/d
   figures are labeled 4Q25 and 1Q26; EIA's AIS limitation must travel with them.
4. **The July 8 IMO page gives an approximate stranded-seafarer/ship statement, not
   a July 15 measurement.** If used at all, attribute it to IMO and date it July 8;
   do not convert “hundreds” or “around/nearly 6,000” into a fresh exact count.
5. **No attacker attribution is verified.** IMO's July 8 and July 13 statements do
   not identify the attackers. Do not add state, military, or proxy attribution.
6. **No aircraft tariff took effect under the July 9 proclamation.** It starts
   negotiations and preserves possible later remedies.
7. **NATO totals are commitments/procurements, not company revenue.** No issuer,
   delivery schedule, margin, or cash receipt follows without a contract or filing.
8. **The existing cyber lane is stale in source coverage.** Its March 2 NCSC alert
   remains only a risk assessment, but a July 13 NCSC/GOV.UK package now confirms an
   active router-exploitation campaign, UK designations, and an official UK/EU
   attribution of a failed December 2025 Polish energy-grid attack. It still does
   not prove a current July outage or successful infrastructure disruption.
9. **The NBS page does not label its timezone.** It displays a July 15 10:00 page
   timestamp; calling that explicitly “China time” is a publisher-context inference
   and should not be presented as a timezone label supplied by the page.
10. **“War premium without broad panic” remains an analytical thesis.** Official
