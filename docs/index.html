the claim becomes a test

Write the actual rules

A YouTube title isn't a strategy. Entry, exit, stop, position size, market, timeframe — if you can't write it down, you can't test it. And neither can they.

One good backtest proves nothing

Any strategy can be curve-fit to look great on history. Robustness testing — walk-forward, Monte Carlo, out-of-sample, real spread and commissions — is what separates an edge from a lucky fit.

Results you can't fake

Every result is recorded and signed, pass or fail. No cherry-picked equity curves. No Photoshopped P&L. What you see is what the test produced.

what the app does with a strategy

1,024 variants go in

Each phase kills the weak ones: bad data, lookahead bias, knife-edge parameters, walk-forward failures, Monte Carlo shuffles, cost stress, regime splits, alpha decay. Most don't make it out. That's the point.

Why one equity curve lies

Resample the trades and one pretty backtest becomes a cloud of possible outcomes. If the strategy only wins in the one history that already happened, it never had an edge.
