- **Affected asset classes:** Sovereign rates, currencies, equities, commodities, and credit.
- **Source types:** National statistical agencies, central banks, government energy agencies, Treasury or debt-management offices, issuer filings, and primary exchanges.
- **Why it remains non-product-dependent:** It is a static carousel, thread, newsletter block, or short video built from public primary evidence and editorial interpretation; it needs no account, dashboard, alerts, recommendations, or finished consumer runtime.

- **Hook:** “A dramatic data print is not a portfolio signal until the confirming assets agree.”

- **Affected asset classes:** A release-specific cross-asset set drawn from rates, FX, equity indexes, commodities, and credit.
- **Source types:** Official releases and calendars, primary-exchange settlement or reference data, Treasury and central-bank series, and issuer filings where relevant.
- **Why it remains non-product-dependent:** It is a repeatable post or weekly scorecard made from public evidence. It does not rely on a portfolio connection, interface, alert engine, recommendation engine, or any unverified product capability.
