# 🎬 SKILL — Realism & Detail
_the texture/lighting cues that make it read as real, not AI. Apply EVERY video. Rules compound as we study more references._

## from: Seedance 4K hyperreal AI short film breakdown (Adil) (2026-07-05T17:43:42.645Z)
- Generate video at 4K, not 1080p — it holds detail into the distance and survives violent dynamics (a flipping vehicle) where 1080p glitches out.
- Showcase the micro-details the model preserves: fabric wrinkles, skin pores, individual eyelashes, veins on hands, glints of light on props.
- Escalate detail on purpose — add small elements (tiny butterflies, abandoned cars, ice barricades) that force richness into the frame.
- Sell physics through secondary motion: snow spray, waterfall mist, impact debris that fades naturally into the distance.
- Add glare and reflections to in-scene screens so they sit in the room's light instead of floating.
- Direct facial emotion (tension, fear, amazement) in the prompt — expressive faces do heavy lifting for realism.

## from: AI commercial/ad workflow (Adil) (2026-07-05T17:53:13.983Z)
- Default the look to bright, clean, high-budget commercial — plasticky or fake-lit locations are unfixable downstream.
- Protect skin texture: never let edited assets replace the original face/skin — mask edits in so the crisp Soul Cinema detail survives.
- Match lighting to location logic: soft, even daylight from the camera side for interiors; for a midday stadium, override to harsh — strong direct sun high and frontal, deep blue sky, crisp hard-edge shadows, saturated color.
- Kill AI-tells in lighting via the prefix: cut contre-jour and shadow-side framing when it buries faces, and fix it globally rather than shot by shot.
- Specify texture-level lighting language (hard-edge shadows, saturation, direction of sun) instead of vague mood words.

## from: Hell Grind AI feature film — 28 pro tips (Higgsfield) (2026-07-05T17:56:56.911Z)
- Ban artificial back-light: "practical light only" (sun, windows, in-frame lamps) is what stops characters reading as pasted-in.
- Write facial anatomy into emotional beats — jaw clenching, eye twitching, mouth open, teeth visible, a tear starting (or explicitly no tears) — this is the fix for robotic AI reactions.
- Bake photographic texture into every prompt: film stock, grain, halation, bokeh, stopped-down aperture — these cues make frames read as shot, not generated.
- Design palettes with 60/30/10 (dominant/secondary/accent) so color reads intentional, not random.
- Hunt these failure modes on sight and regenerate: plasticky/sloppy skin, too much light on faces, off/robotic reactions, cheap-looking VFX elements.

## from: Reference clips — ultra-realism prompt structures (2026-07-05T18:02:10.691Z)
- Imperfection sells realism — explicitly add faded colors, soft contrast, mild sensor noise, mild digital compression artifacts to the prompt.
- Use "realistic skin texture, minimal makeup" on every human subject to counter the AI default of airbrushed perfection.
- Include natural environmental motion detail: moving tree shadows, a light breeze moving loose hair strands, leaves rustling — micro-life signals that break the static-AI feel.
- Match the format aesthetic end-to-end: if the camera is a 2000s DV camcorder, the color, contrast, compression, and motion characteristics must all belong to that same format.

## from: Hell Grind Ep2 — full unedited AI-film process (Adil): style-prefix, 44-iteration locations, diagnosis loop, team collab (2026-07-05T19:07:32.447Z)
- Lock lighting in the style prefix: "natural light only, key light from sky and windows only."
- Add "light atmospheric haze" — one line measurably changes both lighting and texture realism.
- Layer "film grain" and "crush the blacks to add shadow depth" so textures read real instead of plastic.
- Dress the frame with lived-in aging and character-specific lore props — spaces that look inhabited read as filmed.
- Run the slop-vs-cinema check on every output: plastic textures, extra/wrong doors, drifting faces = slop; real texture, moody controlled light, correct geography, intentional camera = cinema.
