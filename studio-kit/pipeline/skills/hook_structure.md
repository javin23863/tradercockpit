# 🎬 SKILL — Hook & Structure
_how the VIDEO itself hooks + holds a viewer (every shot a hook). Apply EVERY video. Rules compound as we study more references._

## from: Seedance 4K hyperreal AI short film breakdown (Adil) (2026-07-05T17:43:42.648Z)
- Treat every single shot as its own hook — a shot you wouldn't watch yourself is a regenerate signal, not a keeper.
- Make shots deliberately harder and more interesting (more subjects, wider scope, extra detail) to manufacture wow-moments.
- Escalate spectacle across the runtime: quiet domestic scene → fantasy flight → wildlife → disaster chase → mass battle.
- Embed close-inspection payoffs (pores, spray, debris) that survive the pause-and-zoom test — reward viewers who look closer.

## from: AI commercial/ad workflow (Adil) (2026-07-05T17:53:13.986Z)
- Structure the script one beat per scene so every shot delivers one clear action and a reason to keep watching.
- Compress mundane actions into a fast tease instead of real time — cut the coffee down to a quick three-stage jump-cut tease.
- Give the product one dedicated hero cut that is nothing but the product, staged with a high-energy move so it lands as a peak, not a pause.
- Button scenes with a striking closing frame — camera low, tilting up so the key element towers — to carry attention into the next cut.

## from: Hell Grind AI feature film — 28 pro tips (Higgsfield) (2026-07-05T17:56:56.912Z)
- Put vision, storytelling, pacing, and cinematography above tools — that human craft is what made a veteran film artist say the AI feature hooked him like a real movie.
- Protect pacing by cutting working material: 110 minutes became 90 by deleting usable generations — "usable" is not the bar; earning its runtime is.

## from: Reference clips — ultra-realism prompt structures (2026-07-05T18:02:10.693Z)
- Make every beat in the timestamped breakdown its own micro-hook — each 2-second segment contains a specific, watchable human action, not a static pose.
