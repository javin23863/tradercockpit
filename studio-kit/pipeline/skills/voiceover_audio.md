# 🎬 SKILL — Voiceover & Audio
_narration/VO + sound that lifts the shot. Apply EVERY video. Rules compound as we study more references._

## from: Seedance 4K hyperreal AI short film breakdown (Adil) (2026-07-05T17:43:42.647Z)
- Generate VO by uploading the finished clip to an AI voice tool and prompting: "analyze the video and create a voiceover in the style of [genre]."
- Choose a voice that matches the genre (e.g., a gravitas documentary voice for wildlife narration).
- Use VO to re-contextualize a shot — documentary-style narration turns a generic animal clip into a documentary scene.

## from: AI commercial/ad workflow (Adil) (2026-07-05T17:53:13.985Z)
- Drop the actual music track into the generation prompt as an input and instruct the performer to move in time with its beat.
- Don't describe rhythm in words — attaching the real track is what makes every move land on the beat.

## from: Hell Grind AI feature film — 28 pro tips (Higgsfield) (2026-07-05T17:56:56.912Z)
- Write dialogue transcripts PHONETICALLY — spell each hard word the way it sounds (e.g. an invented name like "Gandalfina"), not the way it's written.
- Reserve phonetic respelling for complex, invented, or unusual words — that's where it delivers the biggest pronunciation win.

## from: Reference clips — ultra-realism prompt structures (2026-07-05T18:02:10.692Z)
- For UGC realism, specify ambient sound ONLY — name the exact natural sources (morning birds, distant motorcycles, light wind, leaves rustling) so the model or audio layer has a concrete palette.
- Audio must match the visual realism strategy — unproduced DV-camcorder visuals demand unproduced, ambient-only audio; adding music or polished sound design instantly breaks the illusion.
- Apply the audio negative constraints from the master list: "No music. No sound design. No narration." (see `prompting`).

## from: Hell Grind Ep2 — full unedited AI-film process (Adil): style-prefix, 44-iteration locations, diagnosis loop, team collab (2026-07-05T19:07:32.448Z)
- Force the style prefix to environmental sound effects ONLY — no music, no subtitles — at generation time.
- Reason to enforce it: the video model outputs exactly ONE audio track and tends to add music in emotional moments, and you cannot separate music from that track in post.
- Add your own music later, at the film-edit stage, where you control it on its own layer.
- Cut against reference music ("sad music") on the working timeline from the start so pacing decisions preview the final feel.
