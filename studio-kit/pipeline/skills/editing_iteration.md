# 🎬 SKILL — Editing & Iteration
_the ad is the best seconds of 100 tries — cut on action, match cuts, pull keeper phases from many takes. Apply EVERY video. Rules compound as we study more references._

## from: AI commercial/ad workflow (Adil) (2026-07-05T17:53:13.985Z)
- Expect the first generation to fail: run the prompt, diagnose exactly what's wrong, type the fix back to Claude, regenerate.
- Pull keeper phases from different takes — the walk-in from one, the headphones-on-beat from another — and cut the best slices together.
- When one moment inside a shot looks bad, pull it out into its own shot/prompt (1B) with its own angle and cut rhythm instead of fighting the whole scene.
- Iterate on ONE prompt three times rather than fighting three separate ones; sometimes build in pieces, sometimes tweak a single shot until it's perfect.
- Accept the real secret: the finished ad is the best few seconds out of ~100 tries cut together — curation is the final craft.

## from: Hell Grind AI feature film — 28 pro tips (Higgsfield) (2026-07-05T17:56:56.911Z)
- Review every clip FRAME BY FRAME — this is generated video, not footage; one bad frame kills the shot.
- If a 24fps clip stutters at 18 or lower, step through and DELETE the duplicated frames.
- A failed generation isn't waste: 2 seconds of it may be perfect — build scenes from 1–2 second keeper phases.
- Harvest multiple usable phases from one multi-shot generation (grab 4 reactions from one clip) instead of running 4 separate generations.
- Work in TEAMS OF TWO per scene — alone you go slop-blind; a second pair of eyes catches it. And never run only one director, or nobody's left to generate.
- Appoint a SLOP DIRECTOR whose only job is the binary "slop / not slop" call before anything ships — one person caught what fourteen others missed.
- Diff before/after on every fix to confirm the change actually landed.
- Nothing is a dead take: drop a placeholder, keep moving, and regenerate later with fresh eyes and a new prompt.

## from: Reference clips — ultra-realism prompt structures (2026-07-05T18:02:10.692Z)
- Treat the believable shot as 50% prompt, 50% edit — plan the post-production polish before you generate.
- Apply a SPEED RAMP curve on the impact/peak beat: slow → sharp spike → slow, so the hero moment hits and breathes.
- Assemble on an After Effects timeline with motion-tracking points and speed-ramp curves as the core polish stack.
- Use a RESULT vs BEFORE split-frame comparison as your evaluation discipline — judge the polished version directly against the raw generate to verify the edit actually landed.
- Iterate within a single shot by generating new first frames, chaining variants in, and dialing until the convergence clicks — the keeper is the best seconds of many tries.

## from: Hell Grind Ep2 — full unedited AI-film process (Adil): style-prefix, 44-iteration locations, diagnosis loop, team collab (2026-07-05T19:07:32.448Z)
- Start a timeline with reference music on day one and edit alongside generation — you learn what's missing, how shots cut, and how the final will feel before you finish generating.
- Batch 8 videos and SKIM; judge composition first and only watch a take fully if the composition is right.
- If several takes in a batch make the SAME mistake, stop generating and fix the prompt — the error is systematic, not variance.
- Plan for the real hit rate: ~1 in 64 generations is usable and ~1 in 100 makes the final cut — volume is the workflow, not a failure.
- Screenshot a moment you love and use it as a positioning/lighting reference for the next generations.
- Accept scrapping as normal — a script change can kill minutes of finished footage; re-shoot rather than protect sunk cost. That's how you get cinema.
- Keep the shot list as a living two-column doc (scene description | prompt) so week-2 refinement can find and rework any shot.
