# 🎬 SKILL — Camera Angle
_framing that reads cinematic + gives the model depth. Apply EVERY video. Rules compound as we study more references._

## from: Seedance 4K hyperreal AI short film breakdown (Adil) (2026-07-05T17:43:42.643Z)
- Frame interiors at a 3/4 angle to reveal more of the room and give the model depth data that prevents broken geometry.
- Use wide shots that push detail into the far distance — that's where 4K visibly separates from 1080p, which falls apart at depth.
- Start far from the subject and zoom in (e.g., distant wildlife closing to a tight frame) to make static subjects cinematic.
- Override the model's default front-on framing explicitly when it reads flat — dictate the angle you want.
- Cut from a wide establishing shot of a space straight to a tight close-up of a face — the juxtaposition reads as deliberate cinematic continuity.

## from: AI commercial/ad workflow (Adil) (2026-07-05T17:53:13.983Z)
- Frame locations and rooms at 3/4 angle so the model has depth to work with — flat head-on shots kill the win rate.
- Build insert energy from contrast: high-angle close-up straight down (the pour) against worm's-eye looking up (from the burner as the flame catches).
- Use a body-rig snorricam framing for product shots: camera bolted to the body, product rock-solid dead center, everything behind ripping past in motion blur — a thousands-of-dollars rig move, just generated.
- For a closing frame, drop the camera low and tilt up so the key element towers over the character's shoulder.

## from: Hell Grind AI feature film — 28 pro tips (Higgsfield) (2026-07-05T17:56:56.910Z)
- Never frame a location dead-on: 3/4 (tilted, wider) or corner-of-ceiling CCTV angles give the model the depth it needs.
- Start dialogue coverage on a wide or medium establishing frame; push to close-ups only after positions are locked — opening on a close-up makes placement a nightmare.
- Control framing with gear language (specific lens + aperture + distance), not adjectives like "cinematic."

## from: Reference clips — ultra-realism prompt structures (2026-07-05T18:02:10.691Z)
- For UGC, use imperfect framing — off-center, searching compositions that feel caught, not composed; let subjects drift in and out of frame edges.
- For POV shots, frame through the subject's raised arms with environmental elements anchoring the edges (palm trees, building corners) to sell the first-person perspective.
- Describe any phone/camera tilt as an explicit arc ("tilting from pointing straight up to pointing forward") so the model animates the full rotation, not a static angle.

## from: Hell Grind Ep2 — full unedited AI-film process (Adil): style-prefix, 44-iteration locations, diagnosis loop, team collab (2026-07-05T19:07:32.446Z)
- Place the camera INSIDE the room facing inward — never outside looking in — when a character enters; exterior-to-interior entrances confuse the model into inventing doors and teleporting characters.
- Push the camera further into the space to kill "too many doors" artifacts.
- Spell out shot size per beat: "push in from a medium angle to a closeup," "super low-angle closeup on his legs/boots as he steps in."
- Shoot 21:9 for a cinema/big-screen film instead of 16:9.
